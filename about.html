Pictures that I've taken with my telescope:
Celestial Image Celestial Description
Jupiter: The 5th planet from the Sun. It is one of the four gas giants in our solar system. It is the biggest planet in the solar system; so much so that it can fit all the planets inside itself and there's still room to spare. Jupiter is over 700 million kilometers away from the sun and over 500 million kilometers from Earth. This was captured using my telescope and phone, stacked and processed.
Saturn: The 6th planet from the Sun. It one of the four gas giants in our solar system. It is the second largest planet in our solar system and has the biggest ring system of any planet spanning 282,000 kilometers from end-to-end. Saturn is over 800 million kilometers away from the the Sun and over 700 million kilometers from Earth. This was captured using my telescope and phone, single shot.
The Moon: The only satellite (Moon) of Earth. The Moon is the 5th largest satellite in our solar system after IO of Jupiter at diameter of around 3,500 kilometers. It's distance can vary from the Earth, averaging at 384,000 kilometers. This was captured using my telescope and phone, single shot.
