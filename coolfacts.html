Cool Facts

1. Did you know that: Venus is our planets twin, but opposite?!

Venus's axis is tilted 177° degrees from its northern pole, meaning North is its South and South is its North; it basically rotates backwards. Although Venus and the Earth of similar diameters, Venus is over 20x hotter than the Earth at a staggering 475°C average surface temperature compared to Earths' 15°C temperature.

2. Did you know that: Water in the upper troposphere and tropopause doesn't freeze below 0°C?!

As water evaporates from the Earths' surface, water vapours are sent to the upper portion of the troposphere in which clouds are created. After that, either water vapour stays in areas of the atmosphere where temperatures are above freezing or they elevate higher as supercooled water where temperatures are below freezing. This is due to water molecules not completing a process called nucleation.
